After the revolution, the sun felt hotter than normal. Making the first case of people melting around the year 2001, before the towers fell. People started finding that the sun was burning hotter than usual, almost as hot as the winter is as cold and lasted as long as the winter did. Some people who saw people melt in front of their eyes contacted local authorities, which all melted away after an hour passed. Some North Carolina locals who had an underground basement survived the summer.

After 2 years of this new sun called “The radiating death” by locals. The governor, Mike Easley, has given people water limits and emergency cool shelters that are above ground. Which later melted down in 2004 due to 4 weeks of no rain for the summer. Water limits also made people die quicker because the limited water made some underground residents dehydrated in 2 weeks.

Due to this, local events that were supposed to take place during the summer are now strictly banned. Not by law, but by common sense of the community. All other water limits that existed in 2003 were later removed because of the dehydrated deaths.

Legally, the sun is not seen as a weather anomaly that can harm people. But it's seen as the normal sun that has always existed. This can be because the law can't really do anything to prevent the sun from acting up.
